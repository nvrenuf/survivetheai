# AI Chatfishing: How AI Wingmen Are Rewriting Dating App Conversations

You match with someone. The conversation is fast, smooth, and emotionally tuned. They ask the right questions. They flirt without being gross. They “get you.”

It feels like effort.

But there’s a new problem in online dating: the effort might not be theirs.

“Chatfishing” is a cousin of catfishing. Catfishing is about fake identity—fake photos, fake backstory, fake life. Chatfishing targets something more basic: the words. The photos and name can be real, but the messages can be written or heavily shaped by AI instead of typed by the person you matched ([Scientific American](https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/the-rise-of-ai-chatfishing-in-online-dating-poses-a-modern-turing-test/), [BBC](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cvg110ndd8eo)).

That changes the risk. Because text has always been the “proof of work” on dating apps. It was imperfect, but it meant something: time, attention, intent.

Now that signal is getting noisy.

One survey reported that **26% of U.S. singles used AI tools to “enhance dating,” a 333% increase from the prior year** ([Kinsey Institute / Match Group via IU News](https://news.iu.edu/kinseyinstitute/live/news/47848-so-you-fell-for-a-robot-chatfishing-is-taking)).

Even if most of that use is “light help,” not full automation, it’s enough to create a new reality:

**You can’t assume the person you matched is the person writing.**

This post explains what’s happening, how it works, why it matters, and what to do now.

---

## What’s changing (and why it matters)

AI use in dating sits on a spectrum:

- **Light assistance:** rewriting a bio, generating a few openers, polishing tone.
- **Heavy assistance:** AI drafts most replies; the person approves and sends.
- **Proxy conversations:** the chat becomes “AI first,” with the human mostly supervising.
- **Edge cases:** experiments where AI “clones” flirt with other AI “clones” before humans step in ([Scientific American](https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/the-rise-of-ai-chatfishing-in-online-dating-poses-a-modern-turing-test/)).

This is not only about scams. Honest people use AI too—sometimes to reduce anxiety, sometimes to save time, sometimes to compete.

The relationship risk is simpler:

- You invest in a version of someone that may not exist in real life.
- You build attachment to a style of communication they can’t sustain without help.
- You lose time and emotional energy because the early stage was optimized performance.

It doesn’t mean “everyone is lying.” It means the environment rewards the appearance of skill more than the reality of skill—and AI makes that cheap.

## The AI wingman pipeline: how proxy dating works

![AI Chatfishing Funnel Map](/images/chatfishing-funnel-map.png)

### 1) Profile optimization

Some major platforms and companies have discussed or tested AI features that help with photos, prompts, and conversation help ([Business Insider](https://www.businessinsider.com/ai-wingman-dating-helper-tinder-hinge-pickup-lines-chatgpt-2025-12)). Third-party tools also help users rewrite bios, choose photos, and craft prompt answers.

This goes beyond normal “profile advice” because it can create a persona voice that isn’t how the person actually communicates.

**Risk:** you match with an optimized version of someone, then meet the unoptimized version later.

### 2) AI openers

The opener has always been a friction point. AI removes that friction. People paste a match’s profile into a chatbot and ask for a clever first message ([Scientific American](https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/the-rise-of-ai-chatfishing-in-online-dating-poses-a-modern-turing-test/)).

**Risk:** the first impression is no longer a clean signal of effort or interest. It’s a signal that they know how to use tools.

### 3) “Cruise control” conversation

This is where the change becomes relationship-relevant.

Reporting describes “AI wingman” tools that analyze chat context and propose ready-to-send replies, sometimes via screenshots: you upload the conversation, the tool generates a response, you send it ([Business Insider](https://www.businessinsider.com/ai-wingman-dating-helper-tinder-hinge-pickup-lines-chatgpt-2025-12)). At minimum, people keep a chatbot open in a second window and ask what to say next.

**Risk:** you are building rapport with an output system, not a person’s natural communication style.

### 4) Escalation coaching

Some users ask AI when to escalate: when to flirt, when to ask for a number, when to suggest meeting, what to say if there’s silence ([Western University](https://news.westernu.ca/2025/02/ai-dating/)). This can help people who struggle with conversation. It can also turn intimacy into a scripted sequence.

**Risk:** chemistry feels real in text, then collapses in voice or in person.

### 5) Logistics and date planning

Platforms have discussed AI that suggests date ideas or helps users move from chat to meeting ([Business Insider](https://www.businessinsider.com/ai-wingman-dating-helper-tinder-hinge-pickup-lines-chatgpt-2025-12)). That can be useful. It can also keep people in the “chat phase” longer.

**Risk:** more time in the message bubble where emotion grows faster than reality.

## The trust problem: what’s real, what’s performance, what’s outsourced?

Dating apps run on trust signals. Text effort used to be one of them.

Now that AI can create “effort-like” text instantly, the signal is weaker. That doesn’t mean trust is dead. It means **trust has to move to stronger signals earlier**.

This is the same shift you see in security: when passwords fail, you add MFA. When text becomes ambiguous, you add voice and video.

Not because it’s perfect—because it raises the cost of deception and reduces time-waste.

## What to do now: The Trust Ladder protocol

![Trust Ladder Protocol](/images/trust-ladder-protocol.png)

The goal is not to “catch” people. It’s to stop wasting time and emotional energy.

### Step 1: Text (short)

**Goal:** basic alignment (intent, location, timeline).  
**Rule:** do not build deep intimacy in text with someone you haven’t verified.

Signals (not proof, just signals):
- Perfectly smooth replies with zero personal texture
- High emotional intensity early
- Dodging simple specifics (schedule, rough area, realistic details)

### Step 2: Voice (early)

**Goal:** confirm identity and basic presence.  
**Rule:** move to a short voice call before investing.

Script:

> “Quick call? I’ve learned texting can be misleading. Ten minutes is enough.”

### Step 3: Video (when it matters)

**Goal:** match face + voice + real-time interaction.  
**Rule:** if someone wants weeks of daily texting but won’t do a 5–10 minute video call, treat that as meaningful risk.

Script:

> “I don’t want to build momentum on text alone. Can we do a quick video hello?”

### Step 4: Real-world plan (fast, safe, public)

**Goal:** reality check.  
**Rule:** coffee, public place, short duration.

Script:

> “Let’s do a 30-minute coffee this week. If it’s good, we can extend.”

Experts commonly recommend moving to voice/video earlier as a practical verification step because text alone is now too easy to fake or outsource ([BBC](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cvg110ndd8eo), [Western University](https://news.westernu.ca/2025/02/ai-dating/)).

## Claims & Verification

What we can safely claim (from the sources):
- Chatfishing describes AI-assisted romantic messaging that may be undisclosed ([Scientific American](https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/the-rise-of-ai-chatfishing-in-online-dating-poses-a-modern-turing-test/), [BBC](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cvg110ndd8eo)).  
- Survey data reported rapid growth in AI use to “enhance dating” (26% and 333% increase year-over-year in that survey) ([Kinsey Institute / Match Group via IU News](https://news.iu.edu/kinseyinstitute/live/news/47848-so-you-fell-for-a-robot-chatfishing-is-taking)).  
- Major companies and platforms have discussed or piloted AI features for photos, profiles, and conversation help ([Business Insider](https://www.businessinsider.com/ai-wingman-dating-helper-tinder-hinge-pickup-lines-chatgpt-2025-12)).  
- AI text detection tools can be inconsistent, especially in short-form contexts ([ZDNet](https://www.zdnet.com/article/ive-been-testing-ai-content-detectors-for-years-these-are-your-best-options-in-2025/), [Effortless Academic](https://effortlessacademic.com/how-reliable-are-ai-detectors/)).  
- Experts commonly recommend earlier escalation to voice/video as a practical verification approach ([BBC](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cvg110ndd8eo), [Western University](https://news.westernu.ca/2025/02/ai-dating/)).

What’s uncertain:
- How many mainstream dating conversations are “mostly AI” vs “lightly assisted.” Many surveys do not separate levels cleanly ([Scientific American](https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/the-rise-of-ai-chatfishing-in-online-dating-poses-a-modern-turing-test/)).  
- Whether AI assistance improves long-term relationship outcomes; there is limited longitudinal evidence ([Western University](https://news.westernu.ca/2025/02/ai-dating/)).  
- How disclosure rules and labeling will work inside private chats when AI is used as a tool by a human (see EU AI transparency policy discussions: [EU Digital Strategy](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/code-practice-ai-generated-content)).  
- Whether detection and watermarking will become reliable for short messages as models evolve ([ZDNet](https://www.zdnet.com/article/ive-been-testing-ai-content-detectors-for-years-these-are-your-best-options-in-2025/)).
